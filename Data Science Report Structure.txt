Making a report for Data Science:

-Cover Page
  -title of report
  -names of authors
  -contact info
  -name of institutional publisher
  -date of publication
-Table of Contents
  -main headings
  -lists of tables and figures
-Executive Summary/Introduction to Problem
  -explaining the crux of the argument in a brief summary
  -follow up to the summary should be review of relevant
research on the subject matter.
-Methodology
  -introduce research methods and data sources used
  -for new data, explain the data collection exercise in detail
-Results
  -present empirical findings
-Discussion
  -crafting main arguments by building on results
  -where you rely on narrative to take over and enable numbers
to properly communicate my thesis.
-Conclusion
  -generalize specific findings and take on a "marketing approach"
  -also identify future possible developments based on research
and findings.
-References and acknowledgment section
  -list any body of work used in research and findings.
  -list any support offered by those who made work possible.

Report Checklist:

Have you told readers, at the outset, what they might gain by reading your paper?

Have you made the aim of your work clear?

Have you explained the significance of your contribution?

Have you set your work in the appropriate context by giving sufficient background (including a complete set of relevant references) to your work?

Have you addressed the question of practicality and usefulness?

Have you identified future developments that might result from your work?

Have you structured your paper in a clear and logical fashion?
